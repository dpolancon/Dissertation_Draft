# Equation — Chilean Transformation Elasticity θ(ω, φ)

**LaTeX label (canonical):** `ch2:eq:cl_theta`
**Form:** `θ(ω, φ) = θ̃₀ + θ₃·φ + θ₂·ω·φ`

Where:
- `ω` — wage share (as in US case)
- `φ` — BoP-constraint term (capital composition / import dependence)
- `θ̃₀` — baseline transformation elasticity
- `θ₂, θ₃` — BoP interaction terms

## Economic interpretation

The Chilean extension adds the BoP constraint term φ to the US mechanization frontier. This captures the peripheral economy's dependence on imported capital goods: when the BoP tightens (φ rises), the frontier shifts inward, reducing θ and tightening the over-accumulation constraint.

The interaction term θ₂·ω·φ means the BoP effect on mechanization is wage-share-conditional.

## Notes

Contrast with [[eq_theta_omega]] (US case, no BoP term). The identification of θ₂, θ₃ requires the two-cointegrating-vector VECM (CV1: frontier, CV2: BoP equilibrium).
