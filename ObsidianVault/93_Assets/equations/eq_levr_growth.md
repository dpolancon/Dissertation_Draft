# Equation — Leverage Ratio Growth Form

**LaTeX label (canonical):** `ch3:eq:levr_growth`
**Form:** `ĝ_LevR = ĝ_e + ĝ_IR − ĝ_M`

Growth-rate decomposition of the leverage ratio into three components: exchange rate depreciation (ĝ_e), reserve accumulation/decumulation (ĝ_IR), and monetary expansion (ĝ_M).

## Economic interpretation

The growth form shows that LevR deteriorates when monetary expansion (ĝ_M) exceeds reserve accumulation net of exchange depreciation. Under Bretton Woods pegs, ĝ_e ≈ 0, so LevR dynamics reduce to the race between reserve flows and monetary policy.
