# Equation — Leverage Ratio Definition

**LaTeX label (canonical):** `ch3:eq:levr_def`
**Form:** `LevR_t = (e_t × IR_t) / M_t`

Where:
- `e_t` — nominal exchange rate (domestic currency per USD)
- `IR_t` — international reserves (USD)
- `M_t` — monetary base (domestic currency)

## Economic interpretation

The leverage ratio measures the foreign-currency backing of the monetary base. A high LevR means the monetary base is well-backed by international reserves; a declining LevR signals deteriorating external balance and growing BoP constraint.

Under the gold standard / Bretton Woods, a LevR falling below the legal minimum triggers the external constraint (binding condition in [[eq_levr_lom]]).
