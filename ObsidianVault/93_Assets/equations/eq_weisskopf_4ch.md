# Equation — Four-Channel Profit Rate Decomposition

**LaTeX label (canonical):** `ch2:eq:weisskopf_4ch`
**Form:** `r̂ = μ̂ + p̂ + B̂r + π̂`

Where:
- `r̂` — growth rate of the nominal profit rate
- `μ̂` — capacity utilization contribution
- `p̂` — relative price (P_y/P_K) contribution
- `B̂r` — real capital productivity contribution
- `π̂` — profit share contribution

## Economic interpretation

The four-channel identity (Weisskopf 1979, extended) decomposes profit-rate growth into four structurally distinct channels. Each channel maps to a different crisis mechanism in the Basu (2019) taxonomy:

| Channel | Crisis mechanism |
|---------|----------------|
| μ̂ | Demand deficit (realization failure) |
| π̂ | Profit squeeze (distributional conflict) |
| B̂r | Overaccumulation (productivity collapse) |
| p̂ | Relative price deterioration |

## Notes

The identity holds exactly in growth rates (log-differenced form). Peak-to-trough decomposition in [[tab_B2_peaktotrough]] applies this to Fordist profit-rate swings.
