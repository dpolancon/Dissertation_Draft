# Equation — Leverage Ratio Law of Motion

**LaTeX label (canonical):** `ch3:eq:levr_lom`
**Form:** `ΔLevR_t = (CA_t + KA_t) / M_t − LevR_t × ĝ_M`

The law of motion for the leverage ratio. The first term is the BoP-funded accretion of reserve backing per unit of monetary base; the second term is the erosion from monetary expansion.

## Economic interpretation

The leverage ratio is sustainable only if BoP inflows (CA+KA) per unit monetary base exceed the rate of monetary expansion. When this condition fails (ΔLevR < 0 persistently), the external constraint binds.

The binding condition (box equation in the dissertation) is:
`LevR falls below threshold ⟺ ΔLevR < 0 and LevR < LevR_min`

## Notes

This is the boxed "key equation" of Chapter 3. The three historical scenarios (Appendix Table [[tab_ch3_scenarios]]) instantiate this condition for: (A) fiscal monetization pre-UP, (B) UP 1970–73, (C) post-1973 shock therapy.
