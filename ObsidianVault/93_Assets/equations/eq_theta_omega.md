# Equation — Transformation Elasticity θ(ω) — US Case

**LaTeX label (canonical):** `ch2:eq:theta_omega`
**Form:** `θ(ω) = θ̃₀ − θ₁·ω`

The US mechanization possibility frontier expressed as a linear function of the wage share ω. The negative slope θ₁ > 0 means that as the wage share rises (labor costs up), firms adopt more capital-intensive techniques, reducing θ below 1 (over-accumulation territory).

## Economic interpretation

This is the structural identification equation for the US case. At the Harrodian knife-edge ω_H where θ(ω_H) = 1, accumulation is balanced; above ω_H, over-accumulation; below, under-accumulation.
