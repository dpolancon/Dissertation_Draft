# Essay 3: Political Economy of the Unidad Popular

> *Re-visiting the Political Economy of the Rise and Fall of the Unidad Popular: World Money, Class Conflict, and Mechanisms of Dependency*

## Central Argument

This essay contests existing explanations of the UP's rise and fall — classical dependency, Chicago Boys neoliberalism, neo-structuralism, historical political economy — and develops a theoretical framework centered on [[world_money]], [[financial_subordination]], and the [[leverage_ratio]] as a state variable. The BoP-constrained leverage ratio formalizes how peripheral economies face a structural ceiling on accumulation that is independent of internal class conflict, making the UP's collapse analytically distinct from both domestic-only and external-only accounts.
