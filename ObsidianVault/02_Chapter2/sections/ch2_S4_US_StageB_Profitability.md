## Key Arguments — Swing Decomposition (Table B2)
Seven turning points 1951–1974:

| Episode | r̂ | Dominant channel | Interpretation |
|---------|---|-----------------|---------------|
| 1951–54 | −13.3% | π (48.5%), μ (41.7%) | Profit squeeze |
| 1955–58 | −19.1% | μ (60.2%) | Demand-led |
| 1958–66 | +33.6% | All positive: μ (47.7%), Br (22.4%), Py/PK (13.2%), π (16.7%) | Keynesian-Military |
| 1966–70 | −26.9% | Three-channel: π (34.5%), μ (29.8%), Br (29.2%) | Structural crisis |
| 1970–72 | +6.6% | π (52.7%), Py/PK offset (−10.4%) | Partial recovery |
| 1972–74 | −16.4% | **Py/PK (57.8%)**, Br offset (−13.0%) | Oil shock / price-mediated |

## Key Finding
The 1972–74 contraction appears technology-led in the three-channel framework but is price-mediated in the four-channel decomposition. Real capital productivity continued to grow; the oil shock operated through relative prices.
