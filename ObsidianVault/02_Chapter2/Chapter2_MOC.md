# Essay 2: Accumulation, Profitability, and Structural Crisis in Center and Periphery

> *The United States and Chile during the Fordist Era*

## Central Argument

This essay applies Weisskopf's profitability decomposition — extended to a four-channel framework separating relative prices from real capital productivity — to analyze the structural crisis of Fordism (US) and peripheral accumulation under BoP constraint (Chile). The key innovation is the [[transformation_elasticity]] (θ) identification via the Mechanization Possibility Frontier, which allows capacity utilization to be recovered structurally rather than survey-based. The four-channel decomposition reveals that the 1972–74 US contraction was price-mediated (oil shock through relative prices), not a technology failure — a finding suppressed by the three-channel framework.
