# Over-Accumulation

## Definition

A structural tendency in capitalism whereby capital accumulates at a pace that exceeds the expansion of productive capacities to absorb it profitably. Distinguished from *cyclical* underconsumption by its structural, supply-side origin.

## Formal Expression

Captured by the transformation elasticity θ < 1: each wave of capital accumulation expands productive capacities less than proportionally to the capital stock added.

`ΔK → ΔYn < ΔK` (at normal utilization)

## Dissertation Spine (Cosmic Historian annotation, 2026-04-09)

Over-accumulation is the **through-line connecting all three chapters**:

```text
Ch1: θ̂ < 1 as qualitative invariant (scalar, distribution-neutral)
       ↓
Ch2: θ̂(ω) = 0.918 (distribution-conditioned, US)
     θ̂^CL(ω, φ) < 1 (peripherally constrained, Chile — BoP makes trap structural-dynamic)
       ↓
Ch3: Leverage ratio as law of motion — the monetary form through which
     peripheral over-accumulation reproduces itself via the BoP constraint
     and world-money mediation
```

The center-periphery distinction does NOT divide the concept — it intensifies it. At the periphery, θ^CL < 1 is not merely a distributionally-managed stagnation tendency (as in the US): the BoP constraint makes accumulation demand *self-undermining*, converting the over-accumulation tendency from a managed structural pressure into a structural-dynamic trap with no institutional lever capable of relief without external financing.

## Two Expressions of θ < 1

| Economy | Expression | Governing mechanism |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| US (center) | θ̂ = 0.918 at ω̄ = 0.623 | Taylorist compositional upgrading offsets over-accumulation; Fordist institutional settlement manages the distributional channel |
| Chile (periphery) | θ̂^CL(ω, φ) < 1 throughout ISI | BoP constraint converts accumulation demand into import pressure; higher g_K → BoP tightening → μ̂ bounded; no institutional lever that does not worsen the external balance |

The US can manage the tendency through demand activation (military-Keynesian, fiscal management). Chile cannot: the same demand activation that would contain over-accumulation requires imports the BoP cannot sustain.
