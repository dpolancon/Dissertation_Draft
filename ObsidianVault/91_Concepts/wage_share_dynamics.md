# Wage Share Dynamics

## Definition
The time path of the wage share ω (= labour compensation / net output), which is simultaneously:
1. An outcome of distributive conflict (class forces, labour-market tightness, institutional norms)
2. A driver of mechanization strategy (firms adjust capital intensity in response to ω)
3. A regulator of the profit rate through two channels: direct (profit share π = 1−ω) and indirect (via θ(ω))

## The Two Channels Through Which ω Affects r

### Direct channel (profit share)
`π = 1 − ω`
Higher ω → lower π → lower r, holding everything else fixed.

### Indirect channel (mechanization response)
`θ(ω) = θ̃₀ − θ₁·ω`
Higher ω → firms adopt more capital-intensive techniques → lower θ → structural over-accumulation tendency intensifies → B̂r growth slower over time.

These two channels work in the same direction: rising ω is doubly profit-rate-depressing in the long run.

## Fordist Trajectory (US)
| Period | ω dynamics | Effect on θ |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| 1947–1960 | Productivity-indexed wages; ω rising moderately | θ drifts toward ω_H from below |
| 1960–1973 | Full employment erodes reserve army; ω pushes above ω_H | θ < 1; over-accumulation structural |
| 1974–1978 | Oil shock, real wage compression; ω falls back | θ recovery begins; p-channel (Py/PK) dominates crisis |

## Chilean Trajectory
ω dynamics under ISI operate differently: the BoP constraint mediates the pass-through from world prices into domestic wages. The Unidad Popular (1970–1973) represents the sharpest wage-share expansion in the ISI arc — 1972–74 swing: π = 1.381 (π share > 100% of total swing, with μ and B_real jointly offsetting). Post-coup shock therapy inverts ω violently: rapid real wage destruction restores π through the distribution channel, not through productivity gains.

## Path Dependency
The current ω shapes tomorrow's capital stock composition (choice of technique) which shapes tomorrow's B_real and θ. Past distributional outcomes are sedimented into the physical capital stock — each vintage embodies the mechanization choice made at the moment of installation. The capital stock is the accumulated memory of past distributive conflicts.
