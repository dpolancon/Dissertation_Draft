# Accumulation Regime

## Definition
A historically specific configuration of institutional arrangements, distributional norms, demand management practices, and technological paradigms that reproduces profitability and growth over an extended period.

## Fordist Accumulation Regime (US, ~1945–1973)
- **Demand management**: Keynesian fiscal policy + military expenditure (Vietnam)
- **Distribution**: productivity-indexed wages (Fordist wage compromise)
- **Technology**: Taylorist organization of production — systematic deskilling
- **Compensation mechanism**: rising `Br` (real capital productivity) offset falling `μ` and secular `Py/PK` compression

## Dysfunctionality and Crisis
The Fordist regime contained within it the conditions of its own demise:
- Full-employment policy → labour-market tightening → erosion of profit share
- Taylorist deskilling → depletion of cooperative/cognitive labour capacities → exhaustion of productivity gains
- Rising capital accumulation with θ < 1 → over-accumulation tendency intensifies
