# Monetary Expression of Value (MEV)

## Definition
The rate at which abstract labour-time is expressed in monetary units. If one hour of abstract labour produces value monetarily expressed as $x, then MEV = $x per hour. The MEV is not a price — it is the conversion factor between the substance of value (labour-time) and its monetary form (currency units).

## World-Level Determination
Under the dollar standard, the US effectively controls the MEV at the world level. The dollar is the unit of account for international trade, the denomination of commodity pricing (including copper), and the reserve asset peripheral central banks must hold. The peripheral MEV is derivative: it is the domestic MEV *mediated* by the exchange rate and the terms of trade.

For a peripheral economy:
- MEV_domestic = MEV_world × e × (ToT adjustment)
- where e is the nominal exchange rate (domestic per USD)

This means the peripheral economy's capacity to express its own labour-time in monetary form is structurally conditioned by its position in the international monetary hierarchy.

## Historical Regimes
| Regime | MEV anchor | Peripheral implication |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| Gold standard (pre-1914) | Gold weight per currency unit | Fixed MEV; adjustment via gold flows + deflation |
| Bretton Woods (1944–71) | Dollar fixed to gold at $35/oz; periphery pegged to dollar | MEV managed through dollar-peg; peripheral CB must hold dollar reserves → LevR becomes operative |
| Floating (post-1971) | Dollar without gold anchor; US monetary sovereignty | MEV volatile; peripheral currencies absorb valuation shocks → financial subordination intensifies |

## Connection to ζ̂₃ and Financial Fragility
The MEV framing reveals why ζ̂₃ = −3.919 (semi-elasticity of imports to wage share, Ch2 Chile TVECM) carries two dimensions:

1. **Profit-squeeze channel** (standard): Higher ω → lower π → lower accumulation demand → lower imports. This channel operates regardless of MEV determination — it is an income-expenditure identity.

2. **Financial fragility channel** (MEV-mediated): In an economy without [[wage_goods_sovereignty]], rising ω raises import pressure through the social reproduction circuit — wage goods must be purchased at world prices denominated in the reserve currency. The net import effect (−3.919) conceals the internal contradiction between luxury-goods compression (relief on import bill) and wage-goods import pressure (fragility through reserve drainage).

The financial fragility channel is *activated* by the MEV structure: because the peripheral economy cannot express its wage-goods production in its own monetary form at sufficient scale, rising real wages translate into reserve drainage rather than domestic demand multiplication.

## Role in Ch3
The MEV enters Ch3 through S2.2 ([[ch3_S2_2_World_Money_MEV]]). It is the theoretical foundation for understanding why the leverage ratio (LevR = e · IR / M) is not merely a balance-sheet ratio but a monetary expression of the peripheral economy's structural position in the world-money hierarchy.
