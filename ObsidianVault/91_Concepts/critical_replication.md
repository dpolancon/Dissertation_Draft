# Critical Replication

## Definition
A methodological procedure that goes beyond *faithful replication* (reproducing published results exactly) to stress-test the identification strategy across:
- Specification geometry (ARDL lag/lead structure)
- Data construction choices
- System-level coherence (VECM rank, adjustment structure)
- Temporal stability (parameter constancy across sub-samples)

## Four-Stage Protocol in Essay 1
| Stage | Test |
|-------|------|
| S0 | Faithful replication — can the published results be reproduced? |
| S1 | ARDL specification geometry — are results robust to information criteria? |
| S2 | System-level VECM — does cointegrating rank and adjustment structure hold? |
| S3 | Temporal composition audit — is the identification stable across sub-periods? |
