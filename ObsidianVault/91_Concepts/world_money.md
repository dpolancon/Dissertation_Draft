# World Money

## Definition
The money that functions as the universal equivalent in international exchange — historically gold, then the US dollar under Bretton Woods, and the floating dollar post-1971.

## Monetary Expression of Value (MEV)
The rate at which abstract labour-time is monetarily expressed. Under the dollar standard, the US controls the MEV at the world level — peripheral currencies must maintain a fixed or managed relationship, introducing structural subordination.

## Bretton Woods Architecture
- Dollar fixed to gold at $35/ounce (1944–1971)
- Peripheral currencies pegged to dollar
- US surplus recycled through Marshall Plan / cold-war military spending

## Collapse
- Gold window gap: from $35 to $52/oz by 1971 (46.7% gap), $215/oz by 1974 (413.5% rise from 1971)
- Nixon closes gold window August 1971 (unplanned but intentional)
- Unleashes speculative pressures + competitiveness from Japan/Germany (cold-war development)
