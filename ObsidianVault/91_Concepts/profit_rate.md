# Profit Rate (r)

## Definition
`r = π · μ · p · Br`

where:
- `π` = profit share (distribution channel)
- `μ` = capacity utilization (demand channel)
- `p = Py/PK` = relative price of output to capital goods (price channel)
- `Br` = real capital productivity at normal utilization (technology channel)

## Four-Channel Decomposition
`r̂ = π̂ + μ̂ + p̂ + B̂r`

This extends Weisskopf's three-channel framework by separating the output-capital ratio into its price and real components. The standard three-channel framework conflates `p` and `Br` into a single "capital productivity" channel — misattributing price dynamics to technology.

## Historical Pattern (US, 1940–1978)
- **Fordist stability**: r held at 0.187–0.189 across Early and Late Fordist despite declining μ
- **Compensatory mechanism**: rising Br offset falling μ and secular Py/PK compression
- **1966–70 structural crisis**: all four channels simultaneously drag — no offsetting force
- **1972–74 oil shock**: price channel (57.8%) dominates; real Br continued to grow
