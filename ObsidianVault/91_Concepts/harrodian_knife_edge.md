# Harrodian Knife-Edge (ω_H)

## Definition
The wage share ω_H at which the transformation elasticity θ(ω) = 1. At this threshold, capital accumulation expands productive capacities exactly proportionally — no over-accumulation tendency and no excess-demand tendency.

`θ(ω_H) = 1 ⟺ ω_H = (θ̃₀ − 1) / θ₁`

## Interpretation

| Region | θ value | Regime |
|--------|---------|--------|
| ω < ω_H | θ > 1 | Excess effective demand — each unit of deepening mechanization adds capacity faster than capital; short-run tendency toward over-heating |
| ω = ω_H | θ = 1 | Balanced accumulation — Harrodian warranted growth |
| ω > ω_H | θ < 1 | Over-accumulation — mechanization raises capital productivity less than proportionally; structural tendency toward falling r |

## US Empirical Result
For the US NFC sector, Stage A identifies ω_H ≈ 0.617 (labor income share). The post-1960s drift of ω above ω_H places the US in over-accumulation territory — the mechanization tendency becomes structurally profit-rate-depressing.

## Chile: No Knife-Edge Crossing
The peripheral extension reveals that Chile never crosses into excess-demand territory (θ_CL < 1 throughout 1940–1978). The BoP constraint structurally suppresses θ_CL below unity, locking Chile in chronic over-accumulation regardless of ω. The knife-edge is operationally irrelevant for Chile — the BoP tightening preempts any excess-demand phase.

## Both Regimes Short-Run
Short-run: both θ > 1 (excess demand) and θ < 1 (over-accumulation) can occur. The knife-edge marks the long-run structural tendency, shaped by the path-dependency of distributive conflict working through ω.
