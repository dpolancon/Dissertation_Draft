# Reserve Army Elasticity (ρ)

## Definition

The reserve-army elasticity ρ measures the long-run sensitivity of the exploitation rate to capacity utilization within the cointegrating system. It is the parameter that the trivariate VECM *identifies* — in contrast to θ, which the system motivates but cannot endogenize at the bivariate level.

## Key finding (Ch1 S2 VECM)

The exploitation rate accounts for 99% of the long-run variation in the S2 focal specification (p=2, d₂, h₂, r=1). The estimated ρ ≈ −5%: a 1% increase in capacity utilization growth is associated with a 5% decline in the growth of the exploitation rate. This is the reserve-army mechanism operating through the labor market.

## Temporal stability (Ch1 S3)

ρ is invariant to the Fordist/Post-Fordist break: JMN bootstrap LR_obs = 0.363, p_boot = 0.984. The long-run parameter is regime-stable. But the adjustment speed α_y IS unstable (L_c,α = 1.732 vs CV 0.353) — disproportionality is a short-run process.

## Relationship to θ

θ (the transformation elasticity) is the scalar Shaikh estimates. ρ is what the system actually identifies. The identification boundary (Ch1 Conclusion) exists because θ cannot be endogenized without the distributional channel that ρ represents.
