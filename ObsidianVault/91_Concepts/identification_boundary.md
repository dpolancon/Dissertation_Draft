# Identification Boundary

## Definition

The identification boundary is the structural limit beyond which the scalar transformation elasticity θ cannot carry the theoretical weight the framework places on it. It is not a sample limitation but a property of the information set.

## Where it comes from

The four-stage critical replication (Ch1) progressively releases constraints on Shaikh's identification:

| Stage | Constraint released | What it shows |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| S0 | Fixed specification | Benchmark recoverable but bounds test was wrong |
| S1 | ARDL lag order | θ̂ < 1 invariant, but magnitude depends on IC prior |
| S2 | Single-equation | Capacity anchor is a system property; exploitation rate dominates |
| S3 | Full sample | Reserve-army elasticity ρ is regime-stable; adjustment is not |

The boundary exists because the exploitation structure — the mechanism through which distribution drives mechanization intensity and thereby the transformation elasticity — is absent from the bivariate information set.

## What it motivates

The elasticity of productive capacities with respect to accumulation demand is not a finding of Chapter 1. It is the theoretical object the chapter motivates the need for. Endogenizing θ through the distributive channel is the task of Chapter 2.

## The "saturated it" formulation

> "The critical replication has not replaced Shaikh's measurement. It has saturated it — mapping the space of specifications, estimators, and temporal partitions within which the capacity benchmark can be recovered, and identifying the structural boundary beyond which the scalar parameter cannot carry the theoretical weight the framework places on it."
