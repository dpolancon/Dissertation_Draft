# Financial Subordination

## Definition
The structural condition of peripheral economies whereby their access to world money (international reserves, foreign credit) is systematically constrained by their position in the international monetary hierarchy — independent of domestic policy choices.

## Mechanism (Three Channels)

1. Peripheral currencies lack reserve-currency status → balance-of-payments constraint binds
2. Trade and financial flows priced in core-currency → terms-of-trade shocks transmitted via exchange rate
3. External debt denominated in core currency → leverage ratio amplifies external shocks

## The Two-Dimensional Character (ζ̂₃ Finding, Ch2 2026-04-09)

Financial subordination is the structural condition that activates ζ̂₃'s two-dimensional character. The Ch2 Chile TVECM estimated ζ̂₃ = −3.919 (semi-elasticity: ~3.9% import reduction per 1pp wage share increase). This single coefficient conceals two distinct channels:

| Channel | Mechanism | Operates when |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| **Profit-squeeze** (standard) | Higher ω → lower π → lower accumulation demand → lower imports | Always (center and periphery) |
| **Financial fragility** (wage-goods import dependency) | Higher ω → wage-goods import pressure → reserve drainage → BoP binding | Only without [[wage_goods_sovereignty]] (periphery) |

Financial subordination is what makes the second channel operative. An economy with wage-goods sovereignty absorbs distributional gains domestically — rising ω expands domestic demand for domestically produced goods. A financially subordinated economy *transmits* distributional gains into reserve drainage because the wage-goods basket must be imported at world prices denominated in the reserve currency.

This distinction is the structural feature that separates peripheral from center accumulation: **distributive conflict at the periphery has a financial fragility dimension the center does not share**.

### Empirical Anchors from Ch2

- **ζ̂₃ = −3.919**: both channels compound in this coefficient
- **s_π = 1.381**: the UP 1972–74 overdetermination — profit squeeze AND financial fragility operated simultaneously, making the episode self-undermining
- **γ̂ = −0.1394**: TVECM threshold for when the leverage ratio dynamics enter crisis mode — the empirical marker of the financial fragility channel binding

## As a State Variable
Financial subordination enters the model as a state variable (not a parameter) in [[ch3_S2_4_Financial_Subordination_State_Variable]] — its level at any point shapes the feasible space of accumulation. The leverage ratio (LevR = e · IR / M) is the monetary expression of this state variable.
