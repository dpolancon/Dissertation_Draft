# Fordism

## Definition
The dominant accumulation regime in the advanced capitalist core (~1945–1973), characterized by:
- Mass production (Taylorist labour process)
- Mass consumption (productivity-indexed wages)
- Keynesian demand management
- Bretton Woods international monetary order

## Sub-periods in the Dissertation (US)
| Period | Years | Characteristics |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| Early Fordist | ~1947–1958 | Normal cycle, single-channel contractions |
| Late Fordist | ~1958–1966 | "Golden age" — all channels aligned, 1958–66 expansion |
| Fordist crisis | 1966–1974 | Multi-channel structural crisis, Bretton Woods collapse |
| Post-Fordist onset | 1974+ | Oil shock aftermath, structural restructuring |

## Collapse Mechanism
Three simultaneous channels (1966–70):
1. Full-employment → labour-market tightening → profit squeeze
2. Taylorist exhaustion → depletion of productivity gains (Br stagnates)
3. Vietnam fiscal ceiling → demand channel weakens
