# Balance-of-Payments Constraint

## Definition

The structural limit on peripheral growth imposed by the requirement to maintain external balance — i.e., to earn sufficient foreign exchange to finance imports, service external debt, and maintain international reserves.

## Peripheral Twist (Ch2 Session, 2026-04-09)

The BoP constraint is not merely a *ceiling* on growth — it is a **structural-dynamic trap**. The trap operates through accumulation demand itself:

> Higher g_K → higher capital-goods import bill (ζ̂₁ = 0.930 per unit ME accumulation) → BoP pressure → effective demand drained to capital-goods exporters → μ̂ gains bounded → the act of building productive capacity undermines the demand that would activate it.

This is not a distributional ceiling (which policy could adjust by redistributing wage share). It is a structural self-undermining loop specific to economies whose domestic productive structure cannot substitute capital-goods imports at scale — i.e., economies lacking **wage-goods sovereignty** in the capital goods sector.

**Two-phase dynamics:**

1. **Structural/technological trap** — BoP leash on accumulation deepens path-dependently as ISI advances (more machinery → more import requirements → tighter BoP → less capacity activation → worse vintage choices)
2. **Financial crisis** — Bretton Woods collapse (1971) converts latent technological dependency into acute BoP crisis; copper price collapse + dollar float = double-sided P_Y/P_K shock; external shock accelerates but does not create the transition
