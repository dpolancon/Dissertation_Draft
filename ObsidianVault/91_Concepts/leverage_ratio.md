# Leverage Ratio

## Definition
The ratio of external liabilities to external assets (or equivalently, net foreign debt to international reserves) — a balance sheet measure of financial fragility in the international position of a peripheral economy.

## Formal Object
Derived from the balance-of-payments constraint in [[ch3_App_BoP_Leverage_Ratio]]:
- Current account decomposition: trade balance + income balance + transfers
- Capital account decomposition: FDI + portfolio flows + debt flows
- Leverage = f(current account deficit, debt service, reserve drawdown)

## Role in Essay 3
The leverage ratio as a state variable captures the structural ceiling on peripheral accumulation that is independent of internal class conflict. The UP's collapse is analyzable as a trajectory where the leverage ratio crossed a critical threshold — triggered by combined US interventionism, copper price collapse, and Bretton Woods instability.
