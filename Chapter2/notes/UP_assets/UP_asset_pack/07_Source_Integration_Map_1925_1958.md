# Source Integration Map — 1925 to 1958 Backbone

This note shows how the social-history PDF can be used to reinforce the broader Unidad Popular project without letting the early-period material take over the whole paper.

## Why this matters

The 1925-1958 material is not a side story. It gives the deeper historical backbone for three things:
- the institutional formation of the PPNS,
- the anti-communist logic of reformism,
- and the social-historical bridge from rural conflict to urban rupture.

## Best use by section

## Section on the rise of the PPNS (1914-1932)
Use the notes for:
- the 1925 Constitution,
- the creation of the Central Bank,
- Alessandri as statist-reformist anti-communist figure,
- and the impact of Bolshevik fear on elite reform.

### Contribution to master argument
This material helps show that the PPNS did not arise as a neutral developmental apparatus. It emerged as both a modernization project and a containment strategy.

## Section on consolidation and limits (1932-1947)
Use the notes for:
- Ránquil,
- the agrarian question,
- rural repression,
- and the limits of reformism in the countryside.

### Contribution to master argument
This material strengthens the claim that regime consolidation remained shallow because agrarian contradiction was displaced rather than resolved.

## Section on resilience and transition (1947-1957)
Use the notes for:
- anti-communist exclusion,
- urban marginality,
- inflation and social discontent,
- the 1957 rupture as threshold event.

### Contribution to master argument
This section becomes the bridge between the earlier PPNS and the later crisis that opens the road to the 1960s and the Unidad Popular.

## Productive integrations

### Integration A — Alessandri / Recabarren polarity
Use this not as biography for its own sake, but as a stylized contrast:
- reform from above
- organization from below

That polarity helps explain why anti-communist reformism becomes constitutive of the early PPNS.

### Integration B — 1925 institutional architecture
The Constitution and Central Bank material should not sit in isolation. They should be linked explicitly to the question of how the state acquires tools for crisis management and developmental coordination.

### Integration C — Ránquil to 1957
This is perhaps the best medium-range contribution of the notes. The rural and urban conflicts can be used to show that social contradiction shifts terrain without disappearing.

## What not to do

- do not let the 1925-1958 material become a separate history essay detached from the twin-crisis argument;
- do not overextend the displacement thesis into a total explanation of everything after 1957;
- do not treat every institution built in 1925 as already fully equivalent to the later mature PPNS.

## Best writing formula

Use the social-history file in short, high-payoff insertions:
1. one paragraph on institutional formation,
2. one paragraph on anti-communist reformism,
3. one paragraph on agrarian repression,
4. one paragraph on the 1957 threshold.

That is enough to deepen the project without overwhelming it.
