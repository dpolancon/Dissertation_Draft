# Paper Conversion Blueprint

This blueprint converts the presentation into a paper structure that is readable, defensible, and less slide-like.

## Working title

**Revisiting the Political Economy of the Rise and Fall of the Unidad Popular: The Twin Crisis of the Prebischian Populist National State and Global Fordism in Chile**

## Core paper logic

The paper should not open with too much world-historical significance. It should open with the puzzle, then deliver the master claim quickly.

Recommended order:

1. Introduction
2. Theoretical framework
3. Conceptualizing the PPNS within peripheral Fordism
4. Analytical framework: crisis tendencies and regime functionality
5. Historical periodization of Chilean accumulation, 1914-1973
6. The Unidad Popular as condensation of the twin crisis
7. Conclusion

---

## Section-by-section plan

## 1. Introduction

### Goal
State the research question, show the insufficiency of one-sided explanations, and present the twin-crisis thesis.

### Keep
- research question
- core argument
- brief why-it-matters

### Cut or compress
- long bullet list of global significance
- too many consequences before argument appears

### Deliverable shape
Aim for 4 paragraphs:
1. historical puzzle,
2. limits of existing explanations,
3. thesis,
4. roadmap.

---

## 2. Theoretical framework

### Goal
Explain why a variegated-capitalism approach helps and what is distinctively Marxist in your use of it.

### Keep
- critique of methodological nationalism
- institutional coherence / dysfunctionality
- strategic selectivity and world-system mediation

### Add
- one paragraph clarifying distance from standard varieties of capitalism
- one paragraph clarifying relation to dependency theory and regulation theory

### Warning
Do not let this section become a literature parade. It should serve the paper’s machinery.

---

## 3. Conceptualizing the PPNS within peripheral Fordism

### Goal
Define the PPNS and justify the relational account of peripheral Fordism.

### Keep
- comparison tables
- state-form distinctions
- import-substitution and external constraint logic

### Add
- explicit definition box or short subsection titled “What is the PPNS?”
- sentence distinguishing PPNS from generic developmental state and generic populism

### Warning
This section must define, not just describe.

---

## 4. Analytical framework: crisis tendencies and regime functionality

### Goal
Explain how you diagnose regime functionality.

### Keep
- deficit of surplus value vs excess of surplus value
- rising organic composition, profit squeeze, underconsumption, financial fragility
- Weisskopf bridge

### Add
- one compact note explaining indicators behind the table
- one sentence clarifying difference between stagnation tendency and structural crisis

### Warning
This section is where many readers will either trust the architecture or not. Be precise.

---

## 5. Historical periodization, 1914-1973

### Goal
Show the changing capacities of the PPNS across historical phases.

### Keep
- five-period structure
- Table 3
- period summaries

### Revise
- standardize each period with the same internal logic
- move from bullet dumps to mini-arguments

### Best internal sequence for each period
1. accumulation pattern,
2. crisis tendencies,
3. class dynamics,
4. state response,
5. transition.

---

## 6. The Unidad Popular as condensation of the twin crisis

### Goal
Make this the argumentative peak of the paper.

### Keep
- 1970-1971, 1971-1972, 1973 subdivision if useful
- utilization, bottlenecks, sabotage, Bretton Woods, open class struggle

### Add
- explicit statement of what the UP inherited from the prior crisis of the PPNS
- explicit statement of how global Fordist breakdown altered the international field of action

### Warning
Do not leave the twin-crisis articulation implicit here. This section must say the thing out loud.

---

## 7. Conclusion

### Goal
Return to the research question and restate the payoff.

### End on
The coup as violent resolution of a crisis of accumulation, state form, and world-historical restructuring.

### Avoid
- repeating every chronological detail
- drifting into a generic conclusion about neoliberalism

---

## What to cut from the current presentation form

- repeated statements of significance
- long enumerations without analytical sorting
- items marked as hypothesis unless clearly bracketed
- any bullet that cannot be linked to the twin-crisis argument

## What to expand first

- the exact definition of PPNS
- the relation between 1957 and the later UP rupture
- the mechanism linking global Fordist crisis to Chilean domestic crisis
- the operational note behind Table 3

## What can be moved to notes or appendix later

- longer historiographic debates
- indicator construction details
- alternative periodization possibilities
- extended discussion of related Latin American cases

## Drafting discipline

Every major section should end with one bridge sentence saying why that section was necessary for the next one.

That tiny habit will save the paper from turning into a stack of good paragraphs with no engine.
