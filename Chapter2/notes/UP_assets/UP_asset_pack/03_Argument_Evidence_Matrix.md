## Evidence lanes

## Lane A — Conceptual / theoretical
Use for:
- PPNS definition
- peripheral Fordism in relational terms
- twin-crisis articulation
- institutional coherence / dysfunctionality

## Lane B — Macro-historical
Use for:
- profitability patterns
- utilization and output-capital relation
- external constraint
- inflation / disproportionality / realization problems

## Lane C — Social history and class composition
Use for:
- Alessandri / Recabarren polarity
- anti-communist reformism
- Ránquil
- 1957 urban rupture
- peasant and squatter mobilization

## Lane D — World-system / international restructuring
Use for:
- Bretton Woods collapse
- crisis of Atlantic Fordism
- copper, foreign exchange, and terms of trade
- geopolitical harassment and ruling-class strategy

## Red-flag claims to treat as provisional

These should either be sourced more heavily or labeled as working hypotheses:

- the exact causal weight of U.S. military Keynesianism in improving Chile’s terms of trade in the 1960s;
- the precise route by which primitive Taylorism generated Marx-biased technical change in the 1947-1957 phase;
- any strong causal statement linking the global Fordist crisis directly to immediate domestic political outcomes without intermediate mechanisms.

## Best practice for drafting

Each full section of the paper should have:
1. one argument sentence,
2. one mechanism sentence,
3. one evidence sentence,
4. one transition sentence.

That rule should stop the chapter from becoming an overstuffed historical inventory.
