# Conference Handout

## Revisiting the Political Economy of the Rise and Fall of the Unidad Popular
### The Twin Crisis of the Prebischian Populist National State and Global Fordism in Chile

**Diego Polanco**  
University of Massachusetts Amherst  
dpolanconeco@umass.edu

## Research question

What explains the rise and fall of the Unidad Popular?

## Core claim

The Chilean socialist experiment unfolded amid a **twin crisis**:
- the crisis of the **Prebischian Populist National State (PPNS)**, and
- the crisis of **global Fordism**.

The coup of 1973 is therefore interpreted not only as a political rupture, but as the violent resolution of a deeper crisis of accumulation, state form, and world-historical capitalist restructuring.

## Analytical move

The paper uses a Marxist reading of **Variegated Capitalism** to analyze how institutional ensembles differ in their capacity to offset crisis tendencies.

The PPNS is treated as a Latin American developmental state form associated with:
- import-substitution industrialization,
- management of structural heterogeneity,
- external constraint,
- selective social incorporation,
- and political containment of class antagonism.

## Crisis tendencies used in the analysis

### Deficit of surplus value
- rising organic composition of capital
- profit squeeze

### Excess of surplus value
- underconsumption
- financial fragility

## Periodization

| Period | Characterization |
|---|---|
| 1914-1932 | Rise of the PPNS under the dysfunctionality of liberal capitalism |
| 1932-1947 | Consolidation through inward-looking accumulation and unstable ISI |
| 1947-1957 | Resilience under persistent dysfunctionality, inflation, and external constraint |
| 1957-1970 | Crisis of the PPNS and erosion of liberal-democratic stability |
| 1970-1973 | Socialist rupture amid the articulation of domestic and global crisis |

## Main historical payoff

The Unidad Popular did not emerge in an institutional vacuum, nor collapse simply because of domestic polarization or foreign intervention. It condensed a deeper contradiction between a crisis-ridden peripheral developmental state and a transforming world order marked by the breakdown of Fordist stability.

## Current empirical-historical anchors

- 1925 Constitution and Central Bank formation
- Alessandri’s reformism and anti-communism
- Recabarren and the revolutionary counterpole
- Ránquil and unresolved agrarian contradiction
- 1957 as urban rupture and displaced crisis
- Bretton Woods breakdown, external constraint, and sabotage during the UP years
