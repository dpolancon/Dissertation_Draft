# World-Systems and Hegemonic Crisis

## Core idea
National crises are articulated with larger reorganizations of the world economy and of hegemonic order.

## Why useful for Chile
Chile can be read not only as a national case but as part of wider transitions:
- crisis of liberal-export orders,
- postwar reorganization,
- neoliberal globalization,
- and shifts in geopolitical hierarchy.

## Questions
- Did Chile anticipate broader restructuring?
- Was it a laboratory, a frontier, or a dependent adaptation?
- How do national regimes connect to hegemonic transitions?
