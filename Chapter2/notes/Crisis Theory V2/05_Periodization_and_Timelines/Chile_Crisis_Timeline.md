# Chile Crisis Timeline

## Skeleton timeline

### 1. Export-led order and vulnerability
Early externally oriented accumulation with strong dependence on world-market conditions.

### 2. 1930s rupture
Shock to the export order, opening space for state restructuring and developmental responses.

### 3. ISI and developmental contradictions
Industrial expansion with persistent bottlenecks, sectoral imbalance, and foreign-exchange pressures.

### 4. Unidad Popular and rupture
Intensification of class conflict, political polarization, and crisis of governability and accumulation.

### 5. Dictatorship and restructuring
Counter-revolutionary destruction and neoliberal re-foundation.

### 6. 1982 debt crisis
Demonstration that liberalization did not abolish instability.

### 7. Post-1990 neoliberal stabilization
Durable but uneven order combining macro stability with deeper social contradictions.

### 8. 2019 legitimacy crisis
Mass rupture exposing exhaustion of the post-dictatorship settlement.
