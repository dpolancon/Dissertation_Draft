# Overaccumulation

## Core idea
Overaccumulation refers to a condition in which capital has expanded beyond the profitable outlets available for continued valorization.

## Signals
- falling or squeezed profitability,
- idle capacity,
- weak investment despite available funds,
- speculative displacement,
- pressure toward restructuring or devaluation.

## Not a moral category
“Too much capital” does not mean socially too much production. It means too much capital relative to profitable realization and reproduction under existing social relations.

## Chile angle
The concept can be used carefully for moments when:
- profitability weakens,
- external markets constrain expansion,
- debt becomes the bridge that temporarily postpones devaluation.
