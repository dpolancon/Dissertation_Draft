# Mode of Regulation

## Definition
The mode of regulation is the ensemble of norms, institutions, legal forms, state practices, and routines that help reproduce an accumulation regime despite underlying contradictions.

## Distinction
- [[Chapter2/notes/Crisis Theory V2/02_Concepts/Accumulation_Regime]] = pattern of reproduction
- Mode of regulation = institutional and normative supports that help hold that pattern together

## Chile angle
Examples may include:
- labor repression or incorporation,
- macroeconomic policy rules,
- pension architecture,
- central bank framework,
- copper dependence management,
- targeted social policy.

## Diagnostic use
When crisis becomes chronic, ask:
1. Is the accumulation regime itself failing?
2. Or is the mode of regulation failing to stabilize it?
