# Accumulation Regime

## Definition
An accumulation regime is the historically specific pattern through which production, demand, distribution, investment, and reproduction are articulated over time.

## Why it matters
It lets you avoid treating growth as a purely technical process. The regime is stabilized by institutions, norms, state practices, class compromises, and international conditions.

## Typical components
- production structure,
- demand pattern,
- labor relation,
- monetary and financial order,
- state form,
- external insertion.
