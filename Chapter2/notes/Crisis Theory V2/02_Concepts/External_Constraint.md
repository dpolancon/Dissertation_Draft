# External Constraint

## Definition
The external constraint refers to the limits imposed by the balance of payments, foreign-exchange availability, and international dependence on domestic accumulation.

## Why it matters
A national growth process can hit a ceiling not because domestic capacity disappears, but because imported inputs, debt service, or external financing become binding.

## Chile angle
This note is central. Chile’s history repeatedly raises questions around:
- export dependence,
- copper,
- terms of trade,
- foreign debt,
- exchange-rate vulnerability,
- and world-market subordination.

## Good uses
This concept is especially powerful when connecting:
- dependency approaches,
- regulationist periodization,
- and concrete crisis episodes.
