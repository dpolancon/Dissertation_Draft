# Disproportionality

## Core idea
Disproportionality theories stress mismatches among sectors, departments, or components of reproduction.

## Typical forms
- means of production vs means of consumption,
- tradables vs non-tradables,
- wages vs productivity,
- imports vs foreign-exchange earnings.

## Strength
The concept is useful when crisis appears as a breakdown of balance across sectors rather than as a single-variable collapse.

## Chile angle
Especially useful for:
- ISI bottlenecks,
- foreign-exchange shortages,
- sectoral heterogeneity,
- commodity dependence.
